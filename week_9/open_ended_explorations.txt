PHYS 3510 — Spring 2026 — Open-Ended Exploration Suggestions
==================================================
One set of suggestions per exam group topic.

==================================================
GROUP A — Orbital Mechanics
==================================================

1. Three-body figure-eight
   The famous stable three-body solution has all three masses chasing each
   other in a figure-eight. Try to reproduce it by finding the right initial
   conditions and see how sensitive it is to perturbations.

2. Hohmann transfer orbit
   Simulate a spacecraft transferring from a low circular orbit to a higher
   one using two impulsive burns. How does the delta-v cost scale with the
   ratio of the orbit radii?

3. Precessing orbits (GR correction)
   Add a small 1/r^3 correction to Newtonian gravity (a crude GR
   approximation) and watch the ellipse precess. Compare the precession rate
   to Mercury's observed 43 arcseconds per century.

4. Lagrange points
   In a rotating two-body (Earth-Sun) frame, find the five Lagrange points
   numerically by mapping out the effective potential. Which ones are stable?

==================================================
GROUP B — Damped Driven Oscillator
==================================================

1. Poincare sections
   At the driving frequency, sample position and velocity once per period and
   plot the resulting point cloud. How does the section change as you sweep
   the driving amplitude from the linear into the chaotic regime?

2. Bifurcation diagram
   Plot the steady-state amplitude as a function of driving frequency or
   amplitude and look for period-doubling cascades leading to chaos.

3. Energy budget
   Track the power injected by the driving force and the power dissipated by
   damping over one cycle. Do they balance in steady state? What happens
   during the transient?

4. Resonance width vs. damping
   For different damping coefficients, measure the width of the resonance peak
   (FWHM) and check whether it follows the theoretical prediction Q = w0/Dw.

==================================================
GROUP C — Lorenz System
==================================================

1. Lorenz map
   After a long simulation, extract the successive local maxima of Z and plot
   Z_(n+1) vs. Z_n. You should get a nearly one-dimensional tent map —
   explore what this implies about predictability.

2. Fractal dimension
   Use the box-counting method on the attractor to estimate its fractal
   (Hausdorff) dimension. How does it compare to the known value of ~2.06?

3. Parameter space exploration
   Hold sigma and beta fixed at the classic values but sweep rho continuously.
   Map out which values give fixed points, limit cycles, and strange
   attractors. Where exactly does the transition to chaos happen?

4. Coexisting attractors
   For some parameter values (e.g. rho near 13), the Lorenz system has two
   stable fixed points and a strange attractor simultaneously. Can you find
   initial conditions that lead to each? What determines which basin of
   attraction a trajectory falls into?

==================================================
GROUP D — SIR Epidemic Model
==================================================

1. SEIR extension
   Add an Exposed (latent) compartment between S and I. How does the
   incubation period change the shape and timing of the epidemic peak?

2. Vaccination threshold
   Introduce vaccination by removing a fraction of susceptibles at t=0. Find
   the critical vaccination fraction needed to prevent an outbreak and compare
   it to the theoretical herd immunity threshold 1 - 1/R0.

3. Stochastic SIR
   Implement a stochastic version where infections and recoveries are
   Poisson-distributed random events. For a fixed R0, how does the variance
   in peak infection size scale with population N? What happens for very
   small N?

4. Network spreading
   Instead of a well-mixed population, put individuals on a network (e.g. a
   random graph or a scale-free graph) and spread the disease along edges.
   How do network topology and degree distribution change the effective R0?
